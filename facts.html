Fun Facts

Ada was often ill during her childhood.
Ada married William King and was made Earl of Lovelace in 1838, Ada thereby becoming Countess of Lovelace.
Born Augusta Ada King
She was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer
Ada Byron was the only legitimate child of poet Lord Byron and reformer Lady Byron.
Her mother was anxious about her upbringing and promoted Ada's interest in mathematics and logic in an effort to prevent her from developing her father's perceived insanity.
She was buried next to her father at her request.
Her educational and social exploits brought her into contact with scientists
She met Charles Dickens.
Analyst & Metaphysician is what Ada refered to herself as.
She labeled an elaborate set of notes as just 'notes.'
